How is Cancer growing over the years in US?

Relation between count of Cancer Patients and other factors
This project explores the connection between cancer patients counts and other related factors through various interactive visualizations. By interacting with the visualizations the viewer can draw his/her own inferences.

Cancer Count across US
We will see the variation in number of patients diagnosed with cancer in different parts of US.

Cancer in Men VS Women

US Choropleth for Cancer

Cancer across States

The map shows the number of Cancer Patients across different states of the US. The data is plotted over population. It shows there is still a significant difference between the number of Cancer Patients Diagnosed in different States. FurtherMore, If we dig deep down in the statics we will be able to figure out the lifestyle difference, population, food quality and various other factors responsible for variations in the number of Cancer patients, in different States of US.

Interact with the Scatter Plot

Hover over your mouse over the Regions in California, Florida etc., these states have highest number of Cancer Patients. Viewer can draw his/her inferences based on this Choropleth. Hover your mouse over the states in the map to view the state cancer count. The cancer count corresponds to year 2013.

Cancer Count in US Regions Over Years

Life Expectancy vs GDP in 2010

We dive deeper into the relation between Life Expectancy and GDP by observing the scatter-plot and infer that with the per capita GDP increased in various countries, life expectancy increased across various countries and regions of the world in 2010. It looks like there is a correlation between these two elements, year by year GDP per capita grows and Life Expectancy increases. Clearly if you hover your mouse over the Regions in Africa, the countries there have a low life expectancy and low GDP. Viewer can draw his own inferences based on this scatterplot.



Interact with the Scatter Plot

Hover your mouse over the circles to view the country's life expectancy in years on y-axis and per capita GDP on x-axis. The size of the circles is proportional to GDP per capita to remark the difference in the graph. The Regions and countries within that region are shown in the legend. Hover your mouse over regions in the legend to view the countries in that region.

Life Expectancy Across Income Groups

The data is clustered into four income groups as per the dataset – High Income, Upper Middle Income, Lower Middle Income and Low Income. We can observe that there is a significant difference in life expectancy across income groups across all years from 1960 to 2015. As both the Independent and Dependent Variables in this case are Quantitative Continuous, I have taken the line chart to represent the improvement in life expectancy over the years.

Interact with Income Groups

Hover over your mouse over the multi-line chart from left to right and vice versa and observe the life expectancy across the four income groups in the tool-tip. Use each point's tool-tip to get detailed information, just hover over the points in the graph and you can see the life expectancy across the income groups and that year.
