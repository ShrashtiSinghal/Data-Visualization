Cancer Analysis in US

Relation between count of Cancer Patients and other factors
This project explores the connection between cancer patients counts and other related factors through various interactive visualizations. By interacting with the visualizations the viewer can draw his/her own inferences.
Visualize and Draw Inferences to understand the below details.
Over the next few slides we will have the opportunity to explore the data and interact with the visualizations.


#Cancer Count across US
We will see the variation in number of patients diagnosed with cancer in different parts of US through a interactive Choropleth.
#How Age plays a role in Cancer
We will compare the count of cancer patients in various age group through an interactive Bar-graph.
#Types of Cancer
We will see the variation in number of patients diagnosed with different types of cancer in various Years.
#Cancer in Men VS Women
By using a scatterplot and a vornoi diagram, we will see when we hover over an circle, what is the State name, what is the Cancer count in a particular year.

Cancer across States

The map shows the number of Cancer Patients across different states of the US. The data is plotted over population. It shows there is still a significant difference between the number of Cancer Patients Diagnosed in different States. FurtherMore, If we dig deep down in the statics we will be able to figure out the lifestyle difference, polutiontion, food quality and various other factors responsible for variations in the number of Cancer patients, in different States of US.



Interact with the Choropleth

Hover over your mouse over the Regions in California, Florida etc., these states have highest number of Cancer Patients. Viewer can draw his/her inferences based on this Choropleth. Hover your mouse over the states in the map to view the cancer count of the particular State. The cancer count corresponds to year 2013.

Age Factor VS Cancer Count

Does Age plays a role in Cancer?

Bar-graph age range vs Cancer Count

Age Range is plotted on X-axis and Count of Caner Patients is plotted on Y-axis. The Count is the total of a decade from 2005 to 2014. The bar graph clearly shows that Number of Cancer patients do vary in different age range. If we analyse, we see about 80% of people are diagonesed with Cancer in their 40-70 years of Age



Interact with Bar-graph and Button

Hover your mouse over the bars to view the tooltip on Age range and Cancer Count. Click on the Sort button to view Cancer Count for different age range in ascending or descending order.

Types of Cancers

Types of Cancers

The ScatterPlot aside shows the 6 Types of Cancer plotted over years against population. Viewers can draw their own inferences based on this scatterplot. Population from various Years are plotted on X-axis and Count of Cancer Patients diagonosed with different type of Cancers are plotted on Y-axis. Data taken is for years 2000 to 2015. The Scatter Plot will be helpful to understand the major and rare type of Cancer diagosis among the population of US. We See major types of Cancer are Feamale breast Cancer, colon and Rectum Cancer.



Interact with the Scatter Plot

Hover your mouse over the circles to view the type of Cancer in particular year. Count of Cancer Patients is plotted on y-axis and population in Millions on x-axis. The size of the circles is proportional to percentage of count of patients over population. The type of Cancers are shown in the legend. Hover your mouse over Type of Cancer in the legend to view the count of patients in the Scatter Plot of the particular type of Cancer.

Cancer in Male VS Female

Significant differences are visible in number of female and male diagnosed with Cancer over the years 2005 to 2014.

About the visualization


Motivation

The motivation behind this topic is to find the answers of the following questions:
*Is Cancer growing over the years in US?
*How it has changed between men and women in a decade?
*What are the different types of Cancers and their count amoung US Population.
*Why there is a difference between the number of cancer patients diagnosed in different states of US.
*What could be the possible factors responsible for this difference?

This interactive slide show will help the user to infer the answer of the above questions by himself/herself.



,

Tools and Technology Used

Programming : D3.js v4, HTML, CSS
DataBase : csv, json
External APIs : Google fonts, Cloudflare maps
Website Publish: Githib



Visualization Elements

Types of Graphs: Bar, Choropleth, Line, Scatter Plot
Filters: By selecting the values in the legends in the scatter plot diagram, the viewer can filter the type of Cancer
Parameters: In the bar-graph, there is a sort button that sorts the age with respect to highest count of cancer patients in ascending and descending order.
Annotations: All annotations are cleared on each slide and are independent of each other.
Triggers: In the vornoi diagram as the user hovers the mouse over a circle, he/she can see the Cancer Type and Year. The size of the circle represents the percent of count of cancer patients over total population in that year.
ToolTips: When the user hovers over his/her mouse over the graphs, the tool-tips help navigate the data in the visualization.
Theme: The scenes follow a unique theme, which called a dark theme. Dark theme are less tiring on the eyes of users than bright white backgrounds. The theme is uniform for visual consistency.



Conclusion

* Cancer is growing in US per 1000 of population. But the growth is not exponential.
* There were more men suffering from cancer than women in 2005.
* By 2014, more women are suffering from Cancer.
* California, Texas, Florida have the highest number of cancer patients.
* States close to coast have more cancer patients.
*Female Breast Cancer & Colon and Rectum Cancers are the major Cancer Types amoung US Population.
* Most people are diagnosed with Cancer at the age of 50 -60 years
